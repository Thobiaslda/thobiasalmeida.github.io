Case Study: Crisis & Action Planning

Developing and executing a comprehensive crisis management framework for a High-End Real Estate Developer facing a lawsuit and community backlash that threatened to halt a major project.

The Scenario

After securing an environmental license, the client's project was hit with a lawsuit from an NGO, leading to a temporary suspension. This legal action ignited negative media attention and social media chatter, creating a full-blown crisis scenario where misinformation about environmental impacts, transparency, and community exclusion threatened to derail the project entirely.

The Action Plan

I developed a multi-stage action plan that provided the client with a clear roadmap for navigating the crisis. The plan was structured around methodical, concurrent workstreams designed to regain control of the narrative:

  • Information Flow & Approvals: Establishing clear internal channels (WhatsApp, Email) for rapid decision-making.
  • Monitoring & Intelligence: Implementing social media monitoring tools (Buzzmonitor) and mapping key promoters and detractors.
  • Message Construction: Building a master Q&A document to anticipate questions, constructing key messages to highlight project benefits, and developing guidelines for all social media interactions.
  • Targeted Engagement: Creating personalized messaging for different audiences (e.g., residents, activists, leads) and planning sectoral meetings to provide clarifications and emphasize benefits.
  • Content & Channel Strategy: Preparing a multi-channel content plan, including press releases, video pills, and community booklets, to be deployed at key moments.

Results & Impact

The comprehensive plan was successfully implemented, leading to a complete turnaround of the situation and achieving all primary objectives:

✔
License Reinstated
✔
Community Participation
✔
Positive Media Shift
✔
Hostility Neutralized

The project successfully obtained its license to proceed. As a direct result of the targeted engagement and social initiatives, the local population began to participate actively in company-led actions. Media coverage cooled down and became more balanced, while hostile social media profiles faded, replaced by productive dialogue.
